# Phase 07: Counterfactual family expansion

## Entry conditions

- The original family is hardened and its extension interfaces are stable.
- New families have a distinct compiler question, measurable outcome, and source-level enforcement model.

## Candidate families

- Scoped alias relationships between selected memory operations.
- Alignment assumptions with guarded or allocation-level enforcement.
- Loop-invariant and trip-count facts.
- Floating-point reassociation and exception semantics.
- Inlining and visibility constraints.
- Runtime-check threshold and profitability decisions.
- Vectorization-library availability for math calls.
- GCC and Intel optimization-report correlation.
- GPU and accelerator compilation decisions.

## Requirements for every family

- Typed candidate enumeration.
- Isolated compiler delta.
- Stable outcome comparison.
- Family-specific evidence semantics.
- Source-level obligation or explicit non-enforceability.
- Positive, adversarial, and refusal fixtures.
- Security and resource implications.
- Report-schema compatibility.
- Agent repair and rejection guidance.

## Exit gates

A family graduates when it meets the same reproducibility, isolation, identity, semantic-honesty, safe-failure, behavior, and evidence gates as the original noalias family. Expansion may not turn WhyVec into a generic natural-language wrapper around diagnostics.
